Beltline Vendor Zones

A complete guide to legal vendor placement, kiosk locations, and operational rules on the Atlanta Beltline.

What Are Vendor Zones?

Vendor Zones are designated areas along the Atlanta Beltline where approved vendors, kiosks, pop‑ups, and Beltline Cloud stations can legally operate. These zones are created to support local businesses, maintain safe movement flow, and ensure that vendors do not block the trail or interfere with public infrastructure.

Beltline Cloud uses Vendor Zones to organize:

  • ✔ Vendor booths and pop‑ups
  • ✔ Cloud kiosks and information stations
  • ✔ Fast Roll pickup zones
  • ✔ Safety Cloud camera nodes
  • ✔ Workshop and event staging areas

This page explains exactly where vendors can operate, what rules apply, and how Beltline Cloud integrates with the official Beltline vendor system.

Legal Vendor Placement

Vendors may operate in any of the following locations, as long as they follow Beltline rules and obtain the required permits when necessary.

✔ Private Property Adjacent to the Beltline

Vendors may operate on private property that touches the Beltline. This includes storefronts, patios, yards, parking lots, and rooftops facing the trail. This is the easiest and most flexible legal option.

✔ Official Beltline Vendor Pads

The Beltline maintains designated vendor pads in high‑traffic areas such as Old Fourth Ward, Skatepark, Ponce City Market Beltline side, Krog Street, and Westside Trail. These require a Beltline vendor permit.

✔ Business‑Approved Zones

Vendors may operate on property belonging to a business that grants permission. This includes patios, storefront edges, and shared vendor spaces.

✔ Mobile Vendor Booths

Mobile carts, pop‑ups, and rolling kiosks are allowed as long as they do not block the trail, are not attached to Beltline infrastructure, and are not left unattended.

Where Vendors Cannot Operate

To protect public safety and maintain movement flow, vendors may not operate in the following areas:

  • ✘ Beltline poles, lights, fences, or railings
  • ✘ Bridges or narrow trail sections
  • ✘ Middle of the trail or high‑traffic lanes
  • ✘ City of Atlanta or APD Path Force property
  • ✘ Any public infrastructure not designated for vendors

These restrictions ensure that vendor activity does not interfere with safety, emergency access, or Beltline operations.

Beltline Cloud Kiosks

Beltline Cloud kiosks are free‑standing structures that provide information, Safety Cloud access, Fast Roll pickup coordination, and community support. Kiosks may be placed in:

  • ✔ Official vendor pads
  • ✔ Private property adjacent to the Beltline
  • ✔ Business‑approved zones
  • ✔ Mobile kiosk platforms

Kiosks may include:

  • • Safety Cloud cameras
  • • Weather sensors
  • • Charging ports
  • • Cloud information screens
  • • Fast Roll rider check‑in

Kiosks must be free‑standing and may not be attached to Beltline infrastructure.

Camera Placement in Vendor Zones

Vendors and kiosks may legally mount cameras on:

  • ✔ Their own booth or kiosk structure
  • ✔ Their own pole or stand
  • ✔ Private property adjacent to the Beltline
  • ✔ Business‑approved mounting points

Cameras may not be mounted on Beltline poles, lights, fences, or any public infrastructure.

Safety Cloud uses vendor‑zone cameras to monitor:

  • • Crowd density
  • • Parking zone compliance
  • • Night visibility
  • • Hazard detection
  • • Vendor traffic flow
